A good baking sheet is the backbone of nearly every recipe, from cookies to sheet-pan dinners to roasting vegetables. The right pan heats evenly, resists warping, and releases food without a fight. Whether you prefer bare aluminum for browning or a nonstick coating for easy cleanup, the material and construction make all the difference. Most half-sheet pans measure roughly 18 by 13 inches and fit standard ovens. Key factors to weigh: material (aluminum conducts heat best, aluminized steel adds strength), rim design (rolled edges resist warping), and whether you want a coating or a bare surface. Price often reflects gauge thickness and durability, but even budget-friendly options can perform well for occasional use.
